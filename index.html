Flight Data Analysis of a Major Metropolitan Area

Data Source:

For this analysis, flight data related to a major metropolitan area was used. The dataset was sourced from the NYCflights13 package, which provides details about flights departing from NYC in the year 2013.

Summary Statistics & Visual Insights:

1. Distribution of Departure Delays by Airline Carrier:

From this visual, it is evident that:

  • Some carriers have more spread in their delays, indicating variability in their performance.
  • While certain carriers show tight clusters around the median, others have noticeable outliers, with extreme cases of departure delays.

2. Density of Flight Distance vs. Arrival Delay:

Insights derived:

  • Most of the flights have distances less than 2000 miles with arrival delays clustered below 100 minutes.
  • Flights that travel greater distances (more than 3000 miles) seem to have more variability in arrival delays.

3. Top 20 Airports with the Highest Average Delays:

Key Takeaways:

  • CAE, TUL, and OKC are the top three airports with the highest average delays.
  • There is a gradual decrease in delay times as we move down the list, highlighting the disparity between the top delay-prone airports and the rest.

Storytelling & Open Questions:

The presented visuals provide a comprehensive understanding of flight delays, both from an airline carrier and airport perspective. Questions that arise include:

  • Which carriers should passengers potentially avoid if they're wary of delays?
  • Is there a correlation between flight distance and arrival delay?
  • Which airports are notorious for causing delays?
Such questions are crucial for stakeholders, from passengers to airline companies, to make informed decisions.

Conclusion:

This analysis provides a snapshot of flight delays in a major metropolitan area. By identifying carriers and airports with high delays, stakeholders can make better decisions, and relevant authorities can take corrective actions to improve efficiency.
